What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

My pick of the week this week is the Harry Ransom Center, which is located really close to the building in which we have lecture. I went there recently with my French history class to look at the Description of Egypt, a set of volumes about Egypt from the perspective of French scholars and scientists who visited the country with Napoleon from 1798 to 1801. The whole experience is really fascinating, especially since the volumes we got to look at were authentic. Despite their age, the artwork (mostly of architectural structures, flora, and fauna) and technical diagrams we saw were incredible. You can read some more about it here: Wikipedia. A fun fact is that these volumes are absolutely massive, and I don't just mean in terms of the number of pages. The length and width of the books were gigantic. I've never seen anything like it. The Description of Egypt is not open to the public, but if you ever get a chance to see it, don't miss out!
