What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

My pick of the week this week is xG (also known as expected goals). It is a relatively new metric used by soccer analysts to evaluate a team's offensive output. It is very simple to understand (especially if you've read Moneyball (if you haven't, please do)). Whenever a player on a particular team takes a shot towards the opponents goal, computers will look through historical data to find shots similar to the one being taken (for example, similar distance from goal, angle, goalie's position, etc.). Based on the outcomes of these historical goals, the shot is given a score between 0 and 1 (the higher the better). The measure has proven to be very good at predicting scores of matches, and is used to analyze individual players as well. While it has it's limitations (for example, excellent offensive play that does not lead to a shot will not contribute towards a team's xG), it is a very powerful tool for soccer analytics and very interesting to read about.
